This Cyberweek campaign was designed for Sporting Kosovo as a Meta Ads campaign with the primary goal of driving website sales. The campaign included both static and animated creatives, integrating catalog products to scale across multiple items while maintaining a consistent visual identity.

Visual System
Logic

Consistent campaign markers

Cyberweek identifiers were used as recurring visual markers across posts and stories, functioning as badges within the layout. A pixel-inspired aesthetic was adopted as the core visual theme.

Modular layout system

The minimal structure created clear zones for offer, product, and branding, making it easily adaptable to website banners, product posts, and catalog ads across mobile and desktop.

Motion as an extension, not a redesign

The animated creative was developed directly from the static layout, using motion only to enhance hierarchy and attention while preserving the same spacing, structure, and visual language.

Mobile-first readability

Generous white space and controlled typography ensure fast readability on small screens while keeping sufficient focus on catalog products for scroll-based placements.

A key challenge of this campaign was that Cyberweek in this region is traditionally associated with technology brands rather than sports retail. To address this, catalog-based ads were used as the central strategy. Presenting products directly within the Cyberweek visual framework helped break the “tech-only” perception while preserving the recognizable, tech-inspired Cyberweek aesthetic.
